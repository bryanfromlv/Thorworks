Welcome to Thorworks!

I am proud to announce that after months of hard work, thordarsen.net is now live.

My main goal was to make my Forge of Empires tools available to everyone, especially my pride and joy GB Tracker. This tracker has been a labor of love for over a year. While it is a very useful tool for those who play the 1.9 threads, it was an excellent opportunity for me to hone my web development skills.

Many players will find other tools more to their liking. That is fine, I developed my tracker to do what I want within the constraints of time and labor available to me, and InnoGames outright refusal to provide any type of database interface or even a web API to access GB data. Therefore, to use my tool you have to manually input your GB data, such as level, goal, and position rewards. Fortunately, this is only necessary once as the data is saved to your browser's local storage.

While this may seem daunting at first, I think if you give it a try you will find that the short amount of time it takes to enter your GB data is more than worth it for the time and errors it will save you while leveling your buildings. It is important to note that while the tracker works on mobile devices (better on tablet than phone) it was designed to be used on PC where it is easy to switch tabs while entering and updating GB data.The best use case is as I developed and use it, on PC with a dual monitor setup üòÅ

About Thorworks

I developed this website mainly for the Forge of Empires tools, which I developed mostly for learning purposes.

"I" am Thordarsen, my game name for the past 20 years, AKA Alduins Twin in Noarsil (FoE).

The story of my name is more extensive than one might think. When I was a teenager I had a very cool dog whose mother was my first dog, a purebred Collie. I named him Thordarsen J. Meissner. In those days I was starting my career in electronics and worked in a local repair shop fixing TV's and audio equipment. One of the main failures in TV's at that time was the "flyback transformer". Our main supplier for this part was a company named Thordarson Meissner, which is still in business to this day.
